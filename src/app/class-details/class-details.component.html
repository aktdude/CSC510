Assignment #1: Example Assignment

Welcome to your final project for C and Software Tools! Your task is to create a self-driving car using assembly language. This project will require you to draw on all of the skills you've learned throughout the course, including programming, machine learning, and robotics.

Your self-driving car must be able to navigate a pre-defined course without any human intervention. The car should be able to detect obstacles, stop at red lights, and follow the rules of the road. You are free to choose the specific sensors and actuators you use to build your car, but keep in mind that you will be graded on the effectiveness of your design.

To complete this project, you will need to work in teams of 2-3. You will have 3 weeks to complete your self-driving car. During this time, you will be expected to submit regular progress reports, detailing your team's progress and any challenges you've encountered along the way. Your final submission will consist of a demonstration of your self-driving car in action, along with a detailed technical report outlining your design choices and the algorithms you used to create your self-driving system.

In addition to the demonstration and technical report, each team member is required to submit a 10-page essay explaining their individual contribution to the project. This essay should include an overview of the design process, details on the specific programming and machine learning techniques used, and a reflection on what you learned throughout the project.

Please note that you will need to submit both a physical and digital copy of your self-driving car for grading. We will schedule a time for your team to bring your car to campus and demonstrate it in person. Additionally, your technical report and individual essays should be uploaded in a PDF format using the input form provided below.

We encourage you to be creative and ambitious in your design, but remember to prioritize safety and reliability in your self-driving car. Good luck, and have fun building your self-driving car!

Due Date: 4/20/23
